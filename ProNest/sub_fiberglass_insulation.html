Fiberglass Insulation

Product Overview

Fibre Glass insulation, composed of fine glass fibres, is a widely used and cost-effective insulation material. Available in batts, rolls, and loose-fill forms, it effectively reduces heat transfer, improving energy efficiency. While relatively easy to install, proper handling is crucial due to potential skin and respiratory irritation from the fibres. It's commonly used in attics, walls, and floors, providing thermal and some sound insulation.

Why we use Fiberglass Insulation?

Excellent Thermal Performance

Fiberglass insulation is highly effective at reducing heat transfer, keeping your home warmer in the winter and cooler in the summer. This helps to maintain a consistent indoor temperature and reduces the workload on your heating and cooling systems.

Cost-Effectiveness

Compared to many other insulation materials, fiberglass is relatively affordable. This makes it a budget-friendly option for homeowners looking to improve their home's energy efficiency without breaking the bank.

Good Sound Absorption

: In addition to its thermal properties, fiberglass also helps to dampen sound. This can reduce noise transfer between rooms and from outside, creating a quieter and more comfortable living environment.

Fire Resistance

Fiberglass is naturally non-combustible, meaning it won't burn. This adds a layer of fire safety to your home, helping to slow the spread of flames in the event of a fire.

FAQ

Fiberglass is a composite material made of tiny glass fibres bonded together with resin.

Inhaling fiberglass fibres can cause respiratory irritation. It's important to use proper respiratory protection when working with fiberglass insulation.

Fiberglass insulation comes in various forms, including batts, rolls, loose-fill, and rigid boards.
